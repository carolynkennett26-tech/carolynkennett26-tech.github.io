AAG Posters

Conference posters presented at the American Association of Geographers (AAG) Annual Meetings. Each project explores a distinct application of GIS and data visualization in real-world contexts.

Riverside Crime Patterns near Schools, Parks, and Business Districts
(AAG 2025 – Detroit)

This study analyzes how crime clusters around schools, parks, and business districts in Riverside, California. Using spatial analysis tools, the project identifies crime hotspots and compares them with public perception of safety.

  • Data: 2024 local crime reports and student perception survey.
  • Methods: Buffer analysis (300m schools/businesses, 100m parks), spatial joins, and kernel density mapping in ArcGIS Pro and Python.
  • Goal: Inform data-driven strategies for safer community planning.

Tourist Accessibility at Three Roman Heritage Sites
(AAG 2024 – Honolulu)

This poster evaluated accessibility and visitor accommodations at three iconic Roman sites: the Roman Forum, Capitoline Hill, and the Victor Emmanuel Monument. It highlights challenges for mobility-impaired visitors and suggests practical improvements.

  • Scope: Pathways, signage, ramps, elevators, and ticketing access.
  • Methods: On-site observation, spatial mapping of routes and barriers, and qualitative scoring.
  • Goal: Promote more inclusive and sustainable heritage tourism.
