GIS Projects

These are a selection of GIS projects that I created throughout my time at Texas Christian University. Most of these projects come from assignments in GIS classes, but two projects are from an internship I had with Patagonia.

From October 2024 to May 2025, I collaborated with Patagonia Publishing and faculty at TCU to design two interactive ArcGIS StoryMaps based on The Blue Plate: A Food Lover’s Guide to Climate Chaos by Mark J. Easter and Into the Thaw by Jon Waterman. We first read the books, then conducted research and found reliable data to communicate the environmental and economic impacts of climate change. Using ArcGIS Pro and ArcGIS Online, I created custom maps and data visualizations to show regenerative agriculture, wildlife migration, and Arctic warming.

Into the Thaw — Patagonia StoryMap

Environmental storytelling focused on the Brooks Range, Arctic communities, and changing wildlife patterns. For this project, we wanted to immerse the viewer in the Alaskan Arctic. To do this, we combined spatial context, field narratives, audio clips, and curated visuals into one cohesive StoryMap.

  • Tools: ArcGIS StoryMaps, ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Scene Viewer
  • Focus: Narrative mapping, cartographic design

The Blue Plate — Patagonia StoryMap

Explores regenerative farming and its economic and environmental benefits. I mapped practices such as no-till farming and cover cropping and summarized potential carbon and cost savings for producers.

  • Tools: ArcGIS StoryMaps, ArcGIS Online, ArcGIS Business Analyst
  • Focus: Sustainability mapping, data explanation

Riverside Crime Patterns (Schools, Parks, Business Districts)

Crime clusters and safety perception analysis using buffers, spatial joins, and kernel density mapping. Provided solutions for crime monitoring.

This project represented a first draft for my AAG 2025 project. It was the final project for my Urban GIS class in Fall 2024.

  • Tools: ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Online, ArcGIS Business Analyst, Python

Esri Support Analyst Projects

During summer 2024, I had the opportunity to intern with Esri Support Services. During the three-month internship, I had to create three individual projects. I presented each project to the respective Support Analyst team: ArcGIS Online, ArcGIS Desktop, and ArcGIS Enterprise. It was a great learning opportunity to test new tools and concepts that I did not learn inside the classroom.

Furthermore, when I started working full-time at Esri, the new hires had to create an onboarding project to present to all Esri Support Analysts. This was particularly a challenge because we tried to integrate all Esri software: Online, Pro, and Enterprise into one grand analysis.

Unfortunately, Esri Support Services deletes all of its ArcGIS Online content after 90 days, so none of these projects were saved. Read more about them below.

  • Accessibility Mapping Across the U.S. (ArcGIS Online) — Used Survey123 data and Business Analyst demographics to analyze accessibility perceptions in major U.S. cities. Performed spatial joins in ArcGIS Pro and built a StoryMap to visualize differences between perception and actual demographic indicators.
  • Hospital Network Analysis – Pennsylvania (ArcGIS Pro) — Modeled 5-, 10-, and 15-minute drive-time service areas for five cities. Completed route and closest facility analysis for Philadelphia hospitals and pediatric centers to evaluate emergency access and optimal paths.
  • Real-Time Ship Tracking (ArcGIS Enterprise / GeoEvent Server) — Configured GeoEvent to ingest a live JSON feed of ship positions in the San Francisco Bay. Created automated alerts that triggered whenever a vessel crossed a virtual mid-bay boundary.
  • Site Suitability Analysis – Baltimore (Onboarding Team Project) — Worked with a team to evaluate potential locations for a shared workspace company. Used ArcGIS Pro, Business Analyst, and Dashboards to assess transit access, amenities, competitor proximity, flood risk, and crime patterns. Results were delivered through an ArcGIS Dashboards and StoryMaps Briefing.
