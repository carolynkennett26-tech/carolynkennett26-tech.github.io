GIS Projects

Patagonia Publishing Collaboration

These StoryMaps were developed in collaboration with Patagonia Publishing and faculty at Texas Christian University. Each project connects environmental research, spatial data, and storytelling to promote awareness about climate and sustainability. I designed and built the interactive maps and data visualizations for both projects using ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Online, and StoryMaps.

The Blue Plate: A Food Lover’s Guide to Climate Chaos

This StoryMap supports the book *The Blue Plate* by Mark J. Easter and focuses on how regenerative farming can help fight climate change. My map visualizes the environmental and economic benefits of practices such as no-tillage and cover cropping. The StoryMap emphasizes how sustainable agriculture can reduce carbon emissions and improve soil health.

Open StoryMap

Into the Thaw: Exploring Arctic Wildlife and Change

Based on Jon Waterman’s book *Into the Thaw*, this StoryMap follows his journey through Alaska’s Brooks Range. I created spatial layers showing animal migration ranges, temperature change projections, and polar bear sightings. The project highlights how warming conditions are altering Arctic ecosystems and the lives of people and wildlife that depend on them.
